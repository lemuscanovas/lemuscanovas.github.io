Research

Publications, academic profile, and CV of Marc Lemus-Canovas — synoptic climatology, extreme events, attribution, mountain climate.

My research focuses on understanding the link between atmospheric dynamics and extreme meteorological events — heatwaves, compound droughts, and extreme precipitation — with emphasis on the European Alps and Mediterranean region. I develop synoptic classification and attribution methods to quantify the role of climate change in shaping these extremes.

Software

synoptReg — R package for computing synoptic climate classifications and spatial regionalization of environmental data.

Documentation GitHub Paper CRAN

Recognized with the Best Poster Award at the 7th International Conference on Meteorology and Climatology of the Mediterranean (MetMed, 2019). Extended to societal applications through a contract with the Civil Protection of the Aragon Government to define a new nivo-climatic regionalization for their avalanche bulletin. Since May 2025, coordinating an international network (KNMI, IPSL, SLF, Oxford) to develop an R/Python package for extreme weather attribution using the flow analogues technique.
